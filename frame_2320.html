Inserting Images

Welcome to the lesson on adding images to your PowerPoint slides. Including images and visuals can improve the clarity of your presentation and keep your audience engaged. But how do you choose a good image?

When selecting images, look for pictures that have high resolutionThe clarity and detail level of an image, measured in pixels with little to no blurring when resized. They should be relevant to your presentation topic and appropriate for your audience. Avoid using GIFs or moving images, as these can be distracting. Always add alt textA short text description of an image that screen readers read aloud for visually impaired users to every image for accessibility. Pay attention to aspect ratioThe proportional relationship between an image's width and height when resizing to avoid distortion.

Image Insertion Steps

1. Choosing Images

Select images that are clear, relevant to your topic, and appropriate for your audience. Avoid animated GIFs and ensure images are high-resolution.

2. Inserting Images

Navigate to the Insert tab, click Pictures, and choose your image source (device, stock images, or online). Position the image on your slide.

3. Resizing and Repositioning

Click and drag the corner handles to resize proportionally. Use the alignment tools under the Format tab to position your image precisely.

4. Captioning Images

Add a text box below or beside your image to include a caption. Ensure all images include alt text for accessibility (right-click > Edit Alt Text).
